I graduated from Wilson High School and then went to college for business development. While I was in college I left my job as a manager of a sprint store and started working for a bank. I worked my way to the position of Sr. Relationship Manager, Officer. However, I didn't find my life fulfilling so I dropped out of college and recently left my job. Interests and Hobbies

My interests and hobbies are always changing but one that has remained constant for as long as I can remember is standing up for oppressed peoples all over the world. As a Kurd, my family has suffered through systematic oppression, racism, a plethora of human rights violations, and genocide. When I was a child my parents would tell me what my family and people go through. I remember when I was seven and asked my parents if we could go to Washington, D.C. to protest the leader of Iran coming to the United States and they took me. From that point on speaking out on injustices of the world became second nature to me. I also really enjoy volunteer work and helping those in need. During this pandemic, I worked with non-profits and local state/city officials to provide PPE to businesses to keep their employees and the community safe. I also was able to help about fifty busineses stay open by getting grant money to pay for some of their bills and fees. One of my greatest accomplishments was getting 1.3 million dollars to local minority-owned businesses to help them grow in a manageable and eco-friendly way.
